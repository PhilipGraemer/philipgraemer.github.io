About

I study how modern vision architectures including ViTs and CNNs learn, transfer, and fail when moved from natural images into biomedical microscopy. Microscopy is a domain where standard assumptions of computer vision can break down in informative ways. Microscopy can also directly impact people’s lives when used in medicine. It is thus both fascinating and impactful.

My current work centres on a controlled benchmark comparing CNNs and Vision Transformers for single-cell classification on the LIVECell dataset, where I found that previously reported CNN superiority is an artefact of unmatched pretraining. I also found that standard optimisation recipes can unexpectedly hurt performance under domain shift. I have theories for why this might be the case, which I am currently testing empirically.

To build on these findings I am using domain-adaptive self-supervised learning, knowledge distillation, and cross-modality robustness testing.

More broadly, I'm interested in questions beyond benchmarks: What makes one representation transfer better than another? Why do some training recipes fail outside their native domain? How do inductive biases interact with pretraining regime and dataset size?

My path to these questions came through analytical philosophy, formal logic, epistemology, psychology, and neuroscience before arriving at machine learning. I've always been drawn to how systems form knowledge and why they sometimes get it wrong. And I am still interested in how these disciplines can inform ML and how ML can inform them. My study of ML has recently drawn me deeper into mathematics, especially analysis. Because this returns me to terrain close to the formal logic and analytic philosophy with which I began, it feels in some sense like a return to first principles.

Selected results

From a controlled comparison of eleven architectures on LIVECell 8-class single-cell classification, with matched pretraining conditions across all models:

Architecture Accuracy Note
EfficientNet-B5 93.53% Best single model
EVA-02 Base 92.26% Better teacher than EN-B5
EVA-02 → EN-B0 92.67% Cross-arch distillation
EN-B5 → EN-B0 92.18% Same-family distillation
3×EVA-02 Base council → EN-B0 93.42% Council distillation
3×EVA-02 Base council → EN-B0 93.42% Mixed council distillation
ViT-B/16 91.98%
ViT-S/8 92.20% Matches EVA-02 at ¼ parameters, better than the larger ViT-B/16 due to smaller patches/finer tokenisation

Code

livecell-classification-benchmark

Controlled benchmark comparing CNN and Vision Transformer architectures for single-cell classification on LIVECell. Eleven architectures, pretrained and scratch conditions, multi-seed reproducibility.

livecell-distillation

Cross-architecture knowledge distillation. Temperature-scaled soft labels, combined hard/soft loss, council distillation. Didactic codebase with extensive inline documentation.

PyTorch Lightning Knowledge distillation

Validation tool for single-cell crops extracted from segmentation masks. Flags multi-cell crops, boundary truncation, size outliers, and mask coverage issues.
